Frequently Asked Questions

1. What services do you offer?
We provide psychiatric evaluations, medication management, supportive therapy, and telehealth services for individuals dealing with anxiety, depression, trauma, ADHD, and more.
2. Do you accept insurance?
Yes, we accept most major insurance plans. Please contact us directly or check with your provider to confirm coverage.
3. What should I expect during my first appointment?
Your first visit is a judgment-free space to talk about what’s going on. We’ll discuss your medical history, concerns, and goals, and work together to create a treatment plan that feels right for you.
4. How do I book an appointment?
You can schedule online through our secure portal or call our office during business hours.
5. Do you offer telehealth?
Yes, we offer virtual appointments via a secure HIPAA-compliant platform for clients located in Virginia.
6. What if I need to cancel or reschedule?
We ask that you notify us at least 24 hours in advance to avoid a $75 cancellation fee. You can reschedule through the portal or by calling our office.
7. Will I need medication?
Not necessarily. We believe in collaborative care. If medication is appropriate, we’ll discuss it with you openly. If not, we’ll explore other approaches that may support your healing.
8. Is everything I share confidential?
Yes. All sessions are private and confidential, except in situations where disclosure is required by law (e.g., harm to self or others).
9. How soon can I be seen?
We do our best to accommodate new clients quickly. Appointment availability varies, so we recommend scheduling as soon as possible.
10. Can I bring a support person to my session?
Absolutely. If you feel more comfortable bringing a trusted support person, you’re welcome to—just let us know ahead of time.
